How to read the globe

Key ideas

  • Apparent polar wander (APW): The recorded north pole in rocks seems to drift because plates move or the field changes with time.
  • Euler pole-driven plate motion: A rigid plate rotates around a fixed point on the globe (its Euler pole), sweeping continents along arcs.
  • Why compare? One mode moves the land, the other moves the magnetic pole; toggling helps separate plate motion from APW.

Plate motion in action

  • Continents rotate about the Euler pole, carrying paleomagnetic sites with them.
  • The geomagnetic pole marker stays put so you can see continental drift.

Switch to APW-only to freeze the continents and watch the pole migrate instead.
